## 0. What this is and why it is first

S0.1 is the **instrument**: the estimators and the synthetic ground truth that prove they work, before any real or simulated data is interpreted. It is the dependency root of the whole program ‚Äî S0.2 (stability campaign), S0.3 (cascade definitions), and every later stage import it ‚Äî and it is the only Stage-0 slice whose acceptance criterion lives **entirely on synthetic data**, with no dangling external dependency.

Phase 1 delivers exactly **Gate-0(a)** from the plan: *the scaling-relation check distinguishes a critical generator from a non-critical power-law look-alike*, plus recovery of planted `n` and `œÑ`. It maps onto the anti-drift contract's **Gate A** (criticality is earned) and **Gate D** (result-blind selection ‚Äî every threshold here is set on synthetic data / compute budget, never on a result).

**The bounding rule (load-bearing).** Phase-1's stopping criterion is *"recovers planted `n` within CI at the planned event counts,"* **not** "maximally robust at `n=0.99`." The Hawkes likelihood genuinely flattens as `n‚Üí1`; "make the MLE robust at the boundary" is an open-ended optimization trap. The gate is deliberately built to be N-robust (¬ß5) so it does **not** depend on winning that fight. The near-critical regime is *characterized and handed to S0.2*, not gated on here (¬ß9).

## 4. Module specifications

### 4.1 `generators`

**G1 ‚Äî `hawkes_sim.simulate(n, horizon, kernel="exp", backend="auto", rng)` ‚Üí `EventStream`**
- Single exponential kernel `œÜ(t) = n¬∑Œ≤¬∑e^{‚àíŒ≤t}`; branching ratio `n = ‚à´œÜ = Œ±/Œ≤` (here parametrized directly by `n`, with `Œ≤` fixed/configurable). Immigrant (baseline) rate `Œº` configurable.
- **Two backends:**
  - `"thinning"` ‚Äî Ogata thinning. Used for `n ‚â§ 0.9`.
  - `"cluster"` ‚Äî immigrant-cluster (Hawkes‚ÄìOakes) representation: exact for exponential kernels, degrades gracefully where thinning's acceptance rate collapses. Used for `n ‚â• 0.9`. This is the bulk generator S0.2 leans on for the near-critical cells.
  - `"auto"` ‚Äî thinning for `n ‚â§ 0.9`, cluster for `n ‚â• 0.9`.
- **Generator-validity cross-check (required, ¬ß5):** at `n = 0.6` both backends are valid; the harness runs both and asserts agreement.

**G2 + CSN-killer ‚Äî `branching.galton_watson(offspring_mean=m, n_avalanches, rng, size_cap=10**6)` ‚Üí `AvalancheSet`**
- Poisson(`m`) offspring branching process; each tree is one avalanche with size `S` (total nodes) and duration `T` (generations). Simulated generation-by-generation (`Z_{g+1} ~ Poisson(m¬∑Z_g)`), so per-avalanche cost is `O(T)`, not `O(S)`.
- **Size cap + censoring:** the critical (`m=1`) size distribution has infinite mean, so a `size_cap` bounds runaway avalanches; capped avalanches are flagged in `AvalancheSet.censored` and **excluded by the scaling estimators** (their truncated `S` would otherwise bias the tail and the `‚ü®S|T‚ü©` slope). `size_cap=10**6` keeps the censored fraction <0.5% at the harness avalanche counts (verified, ¬ß6); a warning fires if it exceeds 1% (cap too low for the sample).
- `m = 1` ‚Üí **G2 critical**: exact mean-field targets `œÑ = 3/2`, `Œ± = 2`, `1/œÉŒΩz = 2`, with `(Œ±‚àí1)/(œÑ‚àí1) = 2`.
- `m < 1` ‚Üí **CSN-killer (Gate-A.1 tier)**: shares the `s^{‚àí3/2}` body with an exponential cutoff at `s_c ~ 1/(1‚àím)¬≤`; differs from G2 *only in the tail*. S0.1 runs it at `m ‚âà 0.7` (clean, unambiguous CSN rejection). `m` is the exposed knob; S0.2 drives `m ‚Üí 1` (barely-subcritical, hard-to-resolve cutoff) to calibrate the floor.

**Scaling-breaker ‚Äî `lookalike.scaling_break(critical: AvalancheSet, delta, rng)` ‚Üí `AvalancheSet` (Gate-A.2 tier)**
- Permutes the `(S, T)` pairing of a critical `AvalancheSet`. `delta` controls the degree; `delta ‚Üí 1` is the **full shuffle**.
- At full shuffle (S0.1's setting): marginals untouched (A.1 still passes, `œÑ‚âà3/2`, `Œ±‚âà2`), `‚ü®S‚ü©(T)` goes flat ‚Üí measured `1/œÉŒΩz ‚âà 0` ‚Üí `Œî = (Œ±‚àí1)/(œÑ‚àí1) ‚àí 1/œÉŒΩz ‚âà 2`. A **huge, N-robust** violation by design.
- `delta` is the exposed knob; S0.2 drives small-`delta` (a *small* real scaling violation) to calibrate the A.2 informativeness ceiling and false-positive rate against genuine criticality.

> Both look-alikes ship out of S0.1 as **parametrized** generators on G2's GW backbone. S0.1 uses the clean extremes; S0.2 reuses the same generators at near-threshold settings through the same scaling API ‚Äî no rebuild.

### 4.2 `hawkes`

**`mle.fit(events: EventStream, kernel="exp", rng=None)` ‚Üí `HawkesFit(n, params, loglik, ci)`**
- Exponential-kernel log-likelihood via **Ogata's O(N) recursion** (the recursive sum, no O(N¬≤) double loop). `scipy.optimize` for the MLE. `n` reported as the kernel integral.
- **Stationarity-unconstrained:** the optimizer does **not** constrain `n < 1`. The pre-registration treats `n ‚â• 1` as a reportable outcome ("fitted Hawkes may be non-stationary"), so `nÃÇ` may land above 1. This is *why* the CI must be asymmetric.
- **CI = profile-likelihood (primary):** invert the LRT (œá¬≤ threshold); the upper limit is free to exceed 1. Wald/Fisher (symmetric) is **rejected** ‚Äî wrong shape at the boundary. The CI bounds are found by **`scipy.optimize.brentq` root-finding** on `2¬∑(profile_nll(n) ‚àí nll_min) ‚àí œá¬≤‚ÇÅ,‚ÇÄ.‚Çâ‚ÇÖ`, *not* a fine grid scan ‚Äî verified necessary because the sequential Ogata recursion makes a 200-point profile scan (‚âà40k recursion passes) untenable, while root-finding needs ‚âà30 profile evaluations per fit.
- The failure mode being audited at `n = 0.99` is **variance inflation from a flat likelihood**, *not* boundary non-regularity (there is no Chernoff-type œá¬≤ breakdown because `n < 1` is not imposed).

**`residuals.rescaled_residuals(events, fit)` ‚Üí KS diagnostic** ‚Äî time-rescaling (Ogata) residuals; transformed inter-event times should be unit-rate exponential. Phase-1 uses this as a goodness-of-fit readout; Phase-2's `kernel_select` is built on the same machinery.

### 4.3 `powerlaw`

**`csn.fit_powerlaw(x)` ‚Üí `PowerLawFit(exponent, xmin, p_boot, lrt)`; `.passes -> bool`** ‚Äî thin wrapper over the `powerlaw` package: MLE exponent, KS-selected `x_min`, parametric-bootstrap goodness-of-fit `p`, LRT vs lognormal / exponential / truncated power-law. **Gate-A.1 PASS** = bootstrap `p ‚â• p*` **and** no LRT significantly favours an alternative, with **`p* = 0.10` fixed by the CSN (2009) convention** ‚Äî result-blind (a methodological standard, not an inspected result), set now, *not* deferred to S0.2. For the harness's two A.1 cases the verdict is N-robust and invariant to `p* ‚àà {0.05, 0.10}`: the CSN-killer is rejected because the **LRT vs the truncated power law significantly favours the truncated alternative** (its cutoff at `s_c ~ 1/(1‚àím)¬≤` is real and detected), which needs no `p*` at all; G2 passes because no LRT favours an alternative and its bootstrap `p` sits comfortably above `p*`.

### 4.4 `scaling`

**`crackling.exponents(av: AvalancheSet)` ‚Üí `(tau, alpha, inv_snz)`** ‚Äî all three measured the *same way* on the *same* (censored-excluded) avalanche set, so their finite-sample biases stay mutually consistent (that consistency is what the scaling relation tests):
- `œÑ` = CSN exponent of `sizes`; `Œ±` = CSN exponent of `durations` (both via `powerlaw.Fit`, KS-selected `x_min`).
- `1/œÉŒΩz` ‚Äî **estimator OPEN; this is the current Gate-A.2 blocker (see `DECISIONS.md` 2026-06-15).** The naive binned-mean estimator (slope of `log‚ü®S|T‚ü©` vs `log T`) is biased low for a *genuinely critical* generator, so its `Œî` CI **excludes 0** (false-negatives criticality) at every achievable sample size. The duration-`x_min` floor explored as a fix is **rejected** by the anchor check: it was Œî-tuned at rule-selection time (the hole the anti-drift contract exists to catch), the CSN duration `x_min` is N-*unstable* (21‚Üí31‚Üí11 across N=20k/50k/100k ‚Äî noise, not an onset), the binned slope approaches the marginal-implied exponent only as `T‚Üí‚àû`, and at the harness N the resulting floor leaves too few duration bins to fit (numerically brittle). **Leading replacement: the avalanche-shape-collapse estimator (Friedman et al. 2012)** ‚Äî `1/œÉŒΩz` from collapsing per-avalanche *temporal profiles*, the field standard and far less biased ‚Äî but it requires the generators to emit per-avalanche profiles (a generator + `AvalancheSet` extension). **Verify-then-lock** before this module, the ¬ß5 critical cell, plan Tasks 10‚Äì11, or the pre-reg are finalized.

> **Stage-0 pre-plan finding (logged in `DECISIONS.md`):** the binned-slope `Œî` for a genuinely critical generator is `‚âà0.55` at 8√ó10¬≥ avalanches and still `‚âà0.25` (CI `(0.14, 0.46)`, excludes 0; halfwidth `0.16`) at 5√ó10‚Å¥ ‚Äî the failure is point **bias**, not CI width, and it does not vanish at reachable N. This is the false negative S0.1 exists to expose before any real data.

**`crackling.joint_bootstrap(av: AvalancheSet, B, rng)` ‚Üí `DeltaResult(samples, ci, halfwidth)`** ‚Äî the S0.2 interface (¬ß7). Resamples **avalanches** (not exponents) and re-estimates all three exponents per replicate so their correlations are preserved in `Œî`.
> **Hard requirement on the signature:** each replicate re-runs the **full CSN pipeline including KS `x_min` selection**. Fixing `x_min` from the full-data fit and resampling only above it conditions away `x_min`-selection uncertainty and yields a too-narrow `halfwidth` ‚Äî which would bias S0.2's A.2 ceiling tight and make A.2 over-declare "consistent" on real data. This is the precise failure the anti-drift contract exists to prevent.

**`gate_a2.classify(delta: DeltaResult, ceiling=None)` ‚Üí str**
- `ceiling=None` (**Phase 1**): ceiling-free **sign test** ‚Äî `"inconsistent"` if the 95% CI on `Œî` excludes 0, else `"not-inconsistent"`. No trichotomy.
- `ceiling=<float>` (**S0.2**): full trichotomy ‚Äî `"consistent"` (CI contains 0 **and** halfwidth ‚â§ ceiling) / `"inconsistent"` / `"inconclusive"`.

The parameter exists now so Phase-1 imports no S0.2 threshold and S0.2 needs no API change.

## 5. The Gate-0(a) validation harness (`validation/gate0a.py`)

A **2√ó2 truth table**, asserted **deterministically (seeded)**. The harness is built to be **N-robust and calibration-independent** ‚Äî its verdicts do not ride on a CI threshold that is only settled in S0.2.

| Generator (extreme setting) | Gate A.1 (CSN) | Gate A.2 (sign test) |
|---|---|---|
| G2 critical (`m=1`) | **pass** | **not-inconsistent** (CI contains 0) |
| CSN-killer (`m=0.7`) | **reject** | ‚Äî |
| Scaling-breaker (full shuffle) | **pass** | **inconsistent** (CI excludes 0; `1/œÉŒΩz‚âà0`, `Œî‚âà2`) |

- The `Œî‚âà2` violation is *far* outside any CI ‚Äî that hugeness is the **feature** that makes the gate N-robust.
- The scaling-breaker's A.1-pass is **definitionally identical** to G2's (same size multiset after shuffling, same CSN computation) ‚Äî that cell cannot flake.
- The CSN-killer's A.1-reject rests on the **LRT vs the truncated power law** (the `s_c ~ 1/(1‚àím)¬≤` cutoff is real and detected), not on a finely-tuned `p*` ‚Äî N-robust by construction (¬ß4.3).
- **A.2 critical cell is BLOCKED on the ¬ß4.4 estimator.** The scaling-breaker "CI excludes 0" cell (`Œî‚âà2`) is robust under any reasonable `1/œÉŒΩz` estimator. The G2 "CI contains 0" cell is **not** achievable with the binned-slope estimator (it false-negatives criticality, ¬ß4.4) and awaits the shape-collapse replacement; until then this cell is reported **blocked/inconclusive**, not a pass or fail. The harness logs the realized avalanche count and censored fraction in the writedown.
- **Generator-validity cross-check (required):** at `n = 0.6`, run G1 with **both** backends and assert agreement on (i) recovered `n` and (ii) summary event statistics ‚Äî event count, mean inter-event time, avalanche-size histogram ‚Äî within a concrete tolerance: the two backend means agree within `2√ó` the Monte-Carlo SE across the `R` realizations, and the size histograms are not separated by a two-sample test at a fixed level. Rationale: `auto` never exercises both paths on a shared target, yet the unvalidated `cluster` path feeds every high-`n` cell S0.2 builds on (including the ceiling calibration). A silent bug there would corrupt the entire near-critical regime.

**Writedown artifact.** The harness writes a calibration certificate to `results/s0.1_gate0a/<date>_writedown.txt` (the `preflight/` convention from the owner's global checklist): measured `œÑ, Œ±, 1/œÉŒΩz, Œî` and CIs per generator, every pass/fail cell, the backend cross-check deltas, and the `n`/`œÑ` recovery summary (¬ß6). The act of writing the numbers down is part of the gate, not a side effect.

## 6. Acceptance thresholds (the recovery criterion)

Stated as **empirical coverage** against known truth (the right framing for an instrument), not single-realization checks.

**Grid (result-blind ‚Äî set by compute budget only):**
- `n`-recovery (Hawkes): `n ‚àà {0.3, 0.6, 0.9, 0.99}` √ó `N_events ‚àà {1e3, 1e4}`, `R = 100` realizations/cell.
- Monotonicity sanity: `N_events = 1e5` at `n ‚àà {0.3, 0.6}` **only** (not at 0.99 ‚Äî expensive and redundant with S0.2's floor sweep).
- `œÑ`-recovery (GW): `n_avalanches ‚àà {1e3, 1e4}`, `R = 100`.
- Realizations are seed-spawned (`SeedSequence.spawn`) and run via **`multiprocessing`** (embarrassingly parallel; the per-fit Ogata recursion stays sequential).

**`R = 100`** gives coverage SE ‚âà 0.03 ‚Äî enough to separate 0.90 from 0.80, which is all a gate needs.

**Pass criteria (Gate-0(a)):**
1. The 2√ó2 truth table (¬ß5) holds, deterministically.
2. **`n`-recovery:** 95%-profile-CI coverage of planted `n` ‚â• **0.90** at every cell with `n ‚àà {0.3, 0.6, 0.9}` and `N ‚â• 1e4`.
3. **`œÑ`-recovery:** CSN `œÑÃÇ` 95% CI contains 1.5 for critical GW at `n_avalanches ‚â• 1e4`.
4. The `n=0.6` thinning‚Üîcluster backend cross-check passes.
5. Coverage is monotone non-decreasing in `N` at `n ‚àà {0.3, 0.6}` (sanity).
6. The writedown artifact is emitted.

**The near-critical cell (`n = 0.99`) is characterized, not gated.** The harness reports its coverage, CI **width**, and the profile‚Üîbootstrap agreement (below) ‚Äî but its acceptance *threshold* is S0.2's deliverable (the near-critical floor), not a Phase-1 gate. Honest behavior here means: when the instrument cannot resolve `n`, the profile CI is **wide** (low precision), not falsely narrow. Wide-but-covering at `0.99` is correct; coverage falling below 0.90 there is a *finding* written down and handed to S0.2, and is exactly what the audit below interrogates.

**Profile‚Üîbootstrap audit at `n = 0.99` (method transfer, not calibration).** Coverage-vs-ground-truth already certifies whether profile-likelihood is honest at the boundary, so the parametric bootstrap is **not** a calibration check. Its job: the bootstrap is the CI we will use on **real data, where there is no ground truth** (Stage 1). The audit confirms profile and bootstrap **agree on shared datasets at `n = 0.99`**, so the approach carries forward. The writedown records "profile‚Üîbootstrap agreement at the boundary," not "validate profile coverage."

## 10. Deferred to S0.2 (named here so nothing silently imports a missing threshold)

The Gate-A.2 **informativeness ceiling**, the event-count **floor**, the Gate-A.4 **CI threshold**, and the H1c **`delta`** are **all S0.2 deliverables**, set by result-blind synthetic stability simulations that *consume* S0.1's `joint_bootstrap` and parametrized look-alikes. Phase-1 deliberately runs only the regimes where verdicts are unambiguous, so it imports none of them. (The CSN `p* = 0.10` is *not* on this list ‚Äî it is fixed now by the CSN convention; see ¬ß4.3.)
