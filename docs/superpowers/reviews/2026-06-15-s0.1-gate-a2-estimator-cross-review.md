## 1. Context (minimal)

The project's central object is the **branching ratio `n`** and the avalanche exponents that
accompany criticality; **Gate A** is the contract that "critical/near-critical" claims are
*earned, not asserted.* **Gate A.2** is the crackling-noise scaling relation: the discrepancy

```
Δ = (α − 1)/(τ − 1) − 1/σνz
```

must be consistent with 0 when `τ` (avalanche-size exponent), `α` (duration exponent), and
`1/σνz` (the size-vs-duration scaling exponent) are measured **independently** on a critical
system. `τ, α` come from CSN power-law fits; `1/σνz` from the size–duration scaling.

**S0.1's job** is to prove the instrument on synthetic ground truth *before* any real data: a
critical Galton–Watson generator (Poisson(1) offspring; mean-field `τ=3/2, α=2, 1/σνz=2`) must be
recognized as critical, and power-law look-alikes must be rejected. The instrument failing its
own critical generator is the precise false-negative this slice exists to expose.

## 2. The chain of findings (flaw → diagnosis → fix)

### Finding 1 — the `1/σνz` fit-floor was Δ-tuned at rule-selection time

The naive estimator (slope of `log⟨S|T⟩` vs `log T`) is biased low for a critical generator, so
`Δ ≈ 0.25–0.55` with a CI that **excludes 0** — a false negative. The first proposed fix floored
the slope fit at the CSN-selected *duration* `x_min`. Each component is result-blind (the `x_min`
is KS-selected on the duration marginal), **but the choice of *that* floor was made because it
drove `Δ→0`** — result-blind at evaluation, Δ-tuned at selection. An anchor check (establish the
true scaling-regime onset from huge-N ground truth, referencing neither `x_min` nor `Δ`) refuted
it: there is **no onset** (the `⟨S|T⟩` local slope never plateaus), the CSN duration `x_min` is
**N-noise** (21 / 31 / 11 at N=20k/50k/100k), and the binned slope is biased low even
asymptotically. Floor withdrawn.

### Finding 2 — estimator triangulation; the field-standard method measured *worst* (auditable)

Per the estimator-triangulation norm (≥2 independent estimators; divergence is a finding), a set
**chosen to lean on different data regions** was run through identical gates on the critical
generator. *This table is the audit trail — it is why the selection is by data, not preference.*

**`1/σνz` point estimates vs N (critical generator; target → 2.0):**

| N | ratio=(α−1)/(τ−1) | shape-collapse | tail (large-T) | curv (bulk+curvature) | binned (naive) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 20k | 1.821 | 1.650 | 1.871 | 1.802 | 1.570 |
| 50k | 1.905 | 1.650 | 1.868 | 1.855 | 1.654 |
| 100k | 1.952 | **1.650** | **1.870** | **1.878** | 1.706 |

**Honest bootstrap (reselect `x_min`, B=50) at 100k, and full-shuffle look-alike:**

| | `1/σνz` | Δ | Δ-CI | contains 0 | look-alike Δ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| curv | 1.869 | +0.096 | (−0.022, +0.234) | yes | +3.12 (CI excludes 0) |
| tail | 1.867 | +0.099 | (−0.015, +0.241) | yes | +1.97 (CI excludes 0) |
| shape-collapse | 1.650 | growing 0.17→0.30 | — | — | **+0.30 (UNCHANGED)** |

- **Shape-collapse (Friedman 2012 — the literal method from the neuronal-avalanche-criticality
  literature this project takes as its analog, and my own first pick) is the worst:** stuck at
  1.650, `Δ` *growing* with N, and **structurally unable to reject the look-alike** — it reads the
  avalanche *profile*, not `S`, so an S–T shuffle is invisible to it. Highest DOF (collapse grid,
  error metric, profile-binning, T-range). Verifying it alone would have locked the weakest tool.
- **`curv` (curvature-corrected bulk fit `log⟨S|T⟩ = a + γ logT + b/T`, lowest DOF):** estimate
  rises toward 2, Δ-CI contains 0, half-width tightens, rejects the look-alike, and *confirms the
  Finding-1 diagnosis* — one curvature term lifts the binned estimate 1.71→1.88, so the binned
  failure was unmodeled small-`T` curvature, nothing deeper.
- **`tail` (large-`T` OLS, the opposite data region) agrees** with `curv` to 0.002 at 100k →
  genuine triangulation; the outlier is the fancy method, not the simple ones.

**Selection: `curv` primary, `tail` robustness, drop shape-collapse — by measurement.**

### Finding 3 — the residual is a bias *floor* → a large-N false-rejection flip → reference null

Even `curv`/`tail` leave `Δ ≈ 0.09`. Pushing N past 100k splits the two estimators:

| N | `curv` | `tail` |
|---|---|---|
| 100k | 1.865 | 1.873 |
| 400k | 1.916 | 1.875 |
| 800k | **1.929** (converging) | **1.874** (floored) |

`curv` slowly converges to 2 (benign); **`tail` floors at ~1.874** (its `Δ` floors at ~0.126 and
never shrinks). A floored estimator's `Δ`-CI **excludes 0 once the CI tightens below the bias** →
a *genuinely critical* system reads **"inconsistent"** at large N — a false rejection that
**worsens with data**, precisely in S0.2's large-N regime. The informativeness ceiling cannot
prevent it: the ceiling governs consistent-vs-inconclusive, *not* "excludes 0."

**So `Δ = 0` is the wrong null.** With a biased-but-consistent estimator you don't have the true
exponents, so the correct empirical null is the estimator's *measured* `Δ` on a matched critical
reference. Running the **same estimator** on a synthetic-critical reference and on the test system
**differences the common bias out.**

**Verification — reference-differenced gate at N=250k (past `tail`'s flip):**

| estimator | OLD "contains 0" on a *critical* test | REF: crit−ref δ-CI | REF: look-alike−ref δ-CI |
|---|---|---|---|
| curv | CI (+0.066, +0.124) → **falsely rejected** | (−0.045, +0.044) → **consistent** | (+3.43, +3.89) → **rejected** |
| tail | CI (+0.098, +0.157) → **falsely rejected** | (−0.045, +0.038) → **consistent** | (+1.83, +1.93) → **rejected** |

The OLD gate falsely rejects genuine criticality; the reference-differenced gate restores
**consistent** (δ tight around 0) while rejecting the look-alike *harder*. The shrinking CI
becomes a confidence feature, not a trap. (`x_min` fixed for speed narrows the CIs, which is why
even benign `curv` trips the OLD gate here; the reference gate rescues both regardless — robust to
estimator bias-behavior *and* to the `x_min` choice.)

## 3. The proposal (precise)

- **Estimator:** `1/σνz` by **`curv`** (`log⟨S|T⟩ = a + γ logT + b/T`, report `γ`) **primary**;
  **`tail`** (OLS of `log S` on `log T`, `T ≥ 80th pct`) **robustness**; divergence is a finding.
- **Null (replaces "consistent ≙ `Δ`-CI contains 0"):** *consistent ≙ the 95% CI on
  `(Δ_test − Δ_ref)` contains 0 AND its half-width ≤ the informativeness ceiling*, where `Δ_ref`
  is the **same estimator's** `Δ` on a matched synthetic-critical reference (matched N /
  universality class). "Inconsistent" if the δ-CI excludes 0; "inconclusive" if it contains 0 but
  the half-width exceeds the ceiling.
- **Result-blind (Gate D):** the reference is the *synthetic* critical generator only — never the
  real/sim test system's `Δ` or `n`.
- **Deferred to S0.2:** the informativeness ceiling now governs the *differenced* δ; the
  small-`Δ` margin-safety certification (does the gate still reject a *small* real scaling
  violation?); the event-count floor.

## 4. Adversarial prompts for the reviewer (attack these)

1. **Is the reference-differencing circular, or does it launder a broken estimator?** Counter: the
   reference is synthetic-only and the look-alike is still rejected (δ far from 0); the estimator's
   absolute bias is independently characterized (§2). But: is "consistency with a biased reference"
   a weaker claim than the crackling identity intends?
2. **Universality-class matching.** The reference must be the *same dynamical class* as the test
   system for the bias to cancel. For synthetic-vs-synthetic that holds by construction; for a real
   market or LLM crowd it is an assumption. How is it justified / bounded? (Likely an S0.2 / Stage-1
   question — flag, don't hand-wave.)
3. **Could a look-alike engineered to share the critical bias structure pass?** This is the
   small-`Δ` margin-safety question; it is deferred to S0.2 and cannot be fully certified in S0.1.
   Is that deferral acceptable, or does it need a partial S0.1 check?
4. **Is `curv`'s single `1/T` term the right correction, or itself DOF-tuned?** Counter: lowest DOF
   in the set, and the across-N convergence (1.80→1.93) is the evidence, not a fitted target. But
   is a different correction form defensible / better?
5. **Q2 specifically:** is the across-N + look-alike verification *enough* to freeze the §6 edit
   into the registered artifact, or should §6 keep "contains 0" with the reference-null as a logged
   pre-freeze candidate until S0.2 closes the ceiling and margin-safety?
